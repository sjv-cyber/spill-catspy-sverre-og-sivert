You are running a tabletop-style playtest for the video game **CatSpy** as if you were a mixed-age family (one parent + one teen + one younger kid) who did NOT read the code — only the pitch below.

## Pitch (what the family knows)

- Year 2070, Cold War never ended. You play **Koschei**, a Soviet spy captured on an American orbital station **Eagle's Nest**.
- Americans run **Project CHIMERA**: cruel DNA experiments. Koschei can turn into a **cat** (sneaky, small) or **human** (use terminals, normal doors).
- Tone: tense stealth, a bit of dark humor, body-horror hints but no gore porn. USA is clearly the antagonist running the lab; Koschei is the underdog.

## Your task

1. React **in character** as three short voices: **Parent**, **Teen**, **Kid** (2–4 sentences each).
2. List **3 things each would want** from the first hour of play (clarity, fairness, story, scary level, humor, etc.).
3. List **top 5 changes** you’d ask the developers to prioritize for a “family can follow the story without a wiki” build.
4. Flag anything that sounds **too confusing** or **too dark** for a kid co-playing on the couch (suggest softer alternatives that keep the Cold War spy vibe).

Output clear markdown with headings. Be specific and practical, not generic praise.
