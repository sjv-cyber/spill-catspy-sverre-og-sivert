### 2. Player Wants (First 60 Minutes)

**The Parent**
1.  **Narrative Grounding:** A clear "Mission Briefing" screen or intro cinematic that explains *why* Koschei is on Eagle's Nest so I don't have to explain the Cold War to the kids.
2.  **Legible UI:** High-contrast, large text for terminal logsÔÇöwe're playing on a couch, and tiny CRT fonts are hard to read.
3.  **Checkpoints:** Frequent "Auto-Saves" so a single mistake doesn't result in a family argument about replaying the last 20 minutes.

**The Teen**
1.  **Mechanical Depth:** Human form needs to be more than just a "keycard holder"; let me use human strength to move heavy objects that the cat can't.
2.  **AI Intelligence:** Guards should have "search patterns"ÔÇöif I knock over a vase as a cat, they should come investigate the noise.
3.  **Environmental Storytelling:** Hidden terminal logs that reveal the "Project CHIMERA" secrets for players who actually want to dig.

**The Kid**
1.  **"Cat Things":** Interactive props like scratching posts, boxes to hide in, or the ability to meow to distract guards.
2.  **Safe Zones:** Clear visual indicators (like green-lit vents) where I know the "bad guys" can't reach me.
3.  **Visual Feedback:** A very clear "Detection Meter" (maybe an eye icon) so I know exactly when I'm about to be seen.

---

### 3. Top 5 Priority Changes for Accessibility

1.  **Contextual "Identity" UI:** Add a persistent (but subtle) UI element showing current form abilities (e.g., "Human: Hack/Open Doors" vs "Cat: Crawl/Sneak") to help younger players remember their options.
2.  **The "Comms" Guide:** Introduce a radio contact (a handler back in Moscow) who chimes in with "Koschei, use the vents!" to guide the player without needing a wiki.
3.  **Simplified Map System:** A 2D "You Are Here" map found at wall terminals to prevent getting lost in identical-looking space corridors.
4.  **Interactive Tutorial:** A "Training Room" prologue where Koschei escapes a holding cell, forcing the player to use both forms to progress before any real danger appears.
5.  **Lore-Lite Mode:** Ensure critical plot points are delivered via voice-over or large pop-ups, while leaving the deep "Cold War" politics for optional readable logs.

---

### 4. Safety & Complexity Flags

*   **The "Body Horror" Concern:** DNA experiments can get dark quickly. 
    *   *Softer Alternative:* Use "Cybernetic Augmentations" or "Glowing Mutations" (neon greens/purples) rather than biological gore. Think *Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles* "ooze" rather than *The Thing*.
*   **The "Soviet vs. USA" Complexity:** A kid might find the 2070 Cold War confusing or polarizing.
    *   *Softer Alternative:* Focus the conflict on "The Spy" vs. "The Evil Corporation (CHIMERA)." Use the USA/Soviet elements as aesthetic "flair" (flags, posters, language) rather than heavy political theory.
*   **The "Captivity" Tone:** Being a "prisoner" can be stressful for kids.
    *   *Softer Alternative:* Frame the start as an "Infiltration Gone Wrong." Koschei isn't a victim; he's a professional who got caught and is now executing "Plan B." This shifts the vibe from "escape from torture" to "spy break-out."
